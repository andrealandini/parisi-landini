Dynamics and Risks of Carry Trade Strategies in Modern Financial Markets

Published on: 10 November 2024

Authors: Gianni Parisi, Andrea Landini

This paper explores the mechanics, types, and risks associated with carry trade strategies, with a particular focus on recent market reactions to interest rate changes and currency interventions. The study examines how carry trades operate by leveraging interest rate differentials, as investors borrow in lower-yielding currencies and invest in assets with higher returns. It also delves into the various forms of carry trades, including currency and asset-based trades, and highlights associated risks such as currency, interest rate, and liquidity risks. Recent events surrounding the Dollar-Yen carry trade, impacted by Bank of Japan interventions, serve as a case study to illustrate the complexities and potential volatility inherent in these strategies. This analysis aims to provide investors with insights into optimizing carry trades while navigating inherent financial risks.

Stochastic Model & Multivariate Markov-switching GARCH of Gold Price Dynamics

Not Yet Published

Authors: Gianni Parisi, Andrea Landini

This study explores advanced approaches in modeling gold price dynamics using the Stochastic Model and Multivariate Markov-switching GARCH (MSGARCH). These techniques offer powerful tools for capturing volatility patterns, regime shifts, and price dependencies with related assets, such as silver and major currencies. The MSGARCH model, with its regime-switching capability, identifies periods of high and low volatility, allowing for an adaptive view of market fluctuations. Meanwhile, stochastic models with mean-reverting and jump components address the long-term tendencies of gold prices, while also capturing abrupt shifts driven by economic or political events. Future research could enhance these models by integrating machine learning to update parameters dynamically and applying high-frequency HAR models for a finer analysis of intraday price data. Together, these advancements hold potential for deeper insights into gold price behaviors, aiding in strategic investment and risk management.

Modeling of Interest Rate Swaps with Stochastic Volatility and Regime-Switching

Not Yet Published

Authors: Gianni Parisi, Andrea Landini

Interest rate swaps (IRS) are essential tools in financial markets for managing interest rate exposure, providing flexibility in both cash flow management and hedging strategies. This analysis combines advanced stochastic volatility models and regime-switching GARCH frameworks to dynamically capture fluctuations in swap rates, mean reversion, and the volatility clustering often seen in IRS markets.
