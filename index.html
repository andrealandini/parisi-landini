Dynamics and Risks of Carry Trade Strategies in Modern Financial Markets

An In-Depth Examination of Currency, Asset, and Synthetic Carry Trades Amidst Recent Market Volatility
Authors: Gianni Parisi, Andrea Landini

Published on: 10 November 2024

This paper explores the mechanics, types, and risks associated with carry trade strategies, with a particular focus on recent market reactions to interest rate changes and currency interventions. The study examines how carry trades operate by leveraging interest rate differentials, as investors borrow in lower-yielding currencies and invest in assets with higher returns. It also delves into the various forms of carry trades, including currency and asset-based trades, and highlights associated risks such as currency, interest rate, and liquidity risks. Recent events surrounding the Dollar-Yen carry trade, impacted by Bank of Japan interventions, serve as a case study to illustrate the complexities and potential volatility inherent in these strategies. This analysis aims to provide investors with insights into optimizing carry trades while navigating inherent financial risks.
